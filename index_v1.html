About Me

Passionate researcher exploring the intersection of artificial intelligence and robotics

Starting from my studies as a mechanical engineer, I developed a strong interest in artificial intelligence, more precisely machine learning. I am particularly interested in the topic of Deep Reinforcement Learning. Besides theoretical topics of reinforcement learning like new algorithms or problems like artificial curiosity, I am also interested in the application of robotic and autonomous systems like self-driving cars.

I taught myself programming parallel to my studies and completed several online courses including the Artificial Intelligence Nanodegree at Udacity (2020), Deep Reinforcement Learning Nanodegree at Udacity (2019), and Self-Driving Car Engineer Nanodegree at Udacity (2018).

I gained practical experience through an internship at ABB as a machine learning engineer, working on automated stacking and reclaiming systems for open cast mines using reinforcement learning. Subsequently, I completed my master's thesis at ZF on "Optimization of the adaptive control of a damper system/chassis system through fast-learning deep reinforcement learning algorithms".
